Building a project planning system where thinking comes first and time comes later.

At the top, you create a Project. That project has one clear objective — what should exist after 1–2 months. Then you break that objective down into a structured tree: frontend, backend, AI, marketing, whatever. Each of those branches gets broken down again into smaller pieces. You keep drilling down until you reach something small enough to actually do — and that becomes a task.

Tasks are the only things that touch the calendar. Once a task has an owner and a due date, it appears in the calendar view. The calendar is not for planning — it’s only for seeing what must happen on specific days. Planning happens in the tree. Execution shows up in time.

People are simply attached to nodes and tasks, so you always know who owns what. At any moment, you can see:

- The objective (why you’re doing this)
- The structure (how it’s broken down)
- The tasks (what must be done)
- The calendar (when it must be done)
- The owners (who is responsible)

That’s it.

It’s basically a clean flow:

**Goal → Breakdown → Tasks → Dates → Accountability**

No boards. No clutter. No enterprise nonsense.
Just structured thinking connected to time.
